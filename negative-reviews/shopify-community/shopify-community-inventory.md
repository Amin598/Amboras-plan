## Key Patterns

### 1. Variant limits are a deal-breaker
Merchants leave Shopify over the 100-variant cap. It disproportionately hits businesses with physically diverse products (paint colors, fabric prints, jewelry configurations). Workaround apps exist but cost money that eats into margins on already low-profit items. Shopify gates higher limits behind Plus pricing, which feels punitive.

### 2. Multi-location inventory is fundamentally broken
Shopify deducts stock from wrong locations, creates phantom negatives, and has acknowledged bugs it never fixes. This hits hardest at scale (25+ locations). When one location goes negative, it can pull products offline across all channels, meaning a POS glitch at one store can kill online sales.

### 3. Overselling during high-velocity sales
Even with "stop selling when out of stock" enabled, the system allows purchases during flash sales (3-4 orders/min). This is a race condition Shopify has not solved. Merchants discover oversells only after fulfillment fails and customers are angry.

### 4. UI regressions remove critical workflows
Shopify repeatedly ships inventory UI changes that remove columns, hide data, and break bulk editing workflows. Merchants managing thousands of products are forced into product-by-product manual updates or pushed to buy third-party apps to restore previously native functionality.

### 5. Shopify Plus does not fix it
Merchants paying $2,000+/month for Shopify Plus still face the same inventory bugs. New features like higher variant limits are gated behind Plus as a revenue play, not because of technical necessity. This erodes trust across all plan tiers.

## Amboras Opportunity

Every pattern above maps to a core Amboras differentiator:

- **No arbitrary variant limits** -- model products correctly from day one, not as an afterthought or upsell.
- **Real-time, location-aware inventory** -- atomic inventory operations that never create phantom negatives or deduct from wrong locations.
- **Race-condition-proof checkout** -- reserve inventory at cart/checkout time, not at fulfillment time.
- **Inventory UI built for operators** -- bulk editing, filtering, and visibility that treats inventory as a first-class concern, not an afterthought.
- **One tier, no feature gating** -- critical infrastructure like inventory management should work the same on every plan.
