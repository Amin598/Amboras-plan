## Key Patterns

### 1. Fund holds with zero notice or explanation
The single most common complaint. Merchants wake up to frozen funds with no email, no warning, and no stated reason. Shopify provides no proactive communication about why funds are held or what steps the merchant needs to take.

### 2. 120-day holds — and sometimes longer
Shopify can hold merchant funds for 120 days citing "risk of chargeback." Multiple merchants report that even after waiting the full 120 days, funds are still not released. There is no meaningful recourse.

### 3. Support is a black hole
Merchants consistently report speaking to 10+ agents, receiving endless "escalations," and getting zero follow-through. The Trust & Safety team that actually controls payment holds is unreachable by phone. Frontline support is friendly but powerless. Promised callback windows (24-72 hours) are routinely missed.

### 4. Holiday and peak season timing
Shopify triggers compliance reviews during Black Friday, holiday season, and other peak sales periods. This causes maximum financial damage to merchants. Multiple merchants describe this timing as deliberate.

### 5. Existential business threat
This is not an inconvenience. Multiple merchants describe near-bankruptcy, inability to pay suppliers, inability to fulfill orders, and permanent revenue loss. Amounts range from $6,000 to $130,000. Merchants use words like "stolen," "hostage," "theft," and "bankrupt."

### 6. Fraud liability pushed entirely to merchants
Shopify charges chargeback fees even on orders the merchant proactively cancelled. The fraud detection system is described as ineffective. 100% of Shop App transactions in one case were fraudulent. Shopify accepts no accountability.

### 7. No real recourse — only external pressure works
Merchants only get results after filing complaints with the Attorney General, Better Business Bureau, or threatening legal action. Internal support channels cannot resolve payment holds. Some merchants have raised the possibility of class action lawsuits and regulatory complaints (PSD2 violations in Europe).

### 8. Broken verification systems
Automated ID verification fails for valid documents with no manual fallback. Merchants report being stuck in verification limbo for weeks or months. This is a systemic technical issue, not a compliance issue.

---

## Amboras Angle

Every one of these complaints maps directly to the Amboras thesis. Shopify treats payments as a profit center, not a merchant service. The platform:
- Holds funds to reduce its own risk at the merchant's expense
- Has no SLA or KPI for resolving payment holds
- Makes the team that controls funds unreachable
- Punishes merchants for fraud that Shopify's own system failed to catch
- Times compliance reviews for maximum merchant damage

Amboras should treat merchant cash flow as sacred. Transparent payout timelines, real-time hold explanations, proactive fraud detection that protects the merchant (not just the platform), and human-reachable support for payment issues are table-stakes differentiators.
