## Key Patterns

### Plus Paywall
Basic B2B features require $2,000-2,500/month Plus plans with no mid-tier option. Small and medium businesses are completely locked out of native B2B functionality.

### Pricing Inflexibility
Customer-specific, market-specific, and tiered pricing all break across contexts (POS, admin, draft orders, markets). Discounts calculate on wrong price bases. B2B catalogs override market catalogs with no control.

### B2B/B2C Coexistence Is Broken
Tax display, inventory toggles, and catalog overrides cannot be segmented by customer type. Running both B2B and B2C on one store creates constant conflicts.

### Catalog Management Doesn't Scale
No company tagging, no bulk assignment, cumbersome manual processes for thousands of SKUs. Feature requests go unanswered for over a year.

### Draft Orders Are Unreliable
Pricing changes unexpectedly at checkout, checkout rules don't apply, and deleted drafts remain purchasable. Breaks the quote-to-order workflow that B2B depends on.

### POS Is Completely Disconnected
B2B pricing doesn't carry over to in-store sales at all. No API support for developers to build solutions. Staff must manually look up and adjust prices.

### Payment Workflows Are Primitive
No card-on-file for phone orders, no consolidated invoicing, no native net terms without third-party apps. Basic B2B payment patterns are unsupported.

---

## Amboras Implications

Every pattern above maps directly to the Amboras thesis: Shopify gives you tools but doesn't help you sell. For B2B specifically, Shopify doesn't even give you the tools -- it gives you a $2,500/month paywall and a half-built feature set behind it. An AI-native platform that treats B2B as a first-class citizen (unified pricing engine, smart catalogs, integrated invoicing, POS parity) would have a massive opening in this market.
